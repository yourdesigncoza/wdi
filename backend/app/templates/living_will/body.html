{# --- Important Notes --- #}

Important Notes

  • It is recommended that you discuss the contents of this directive with a medical professional to ensure you fully understand the implications of your treatment preferences.
  • Please provide copies of this directive to your healthcare proxy, family members, personal physician, and any hospital or care facility where you may receive treatment.
  • While South Africa does not have specific legislation governing living wills, this directive is supported by constitutional rights to dignity (Section 10) and bodily integrity (Section 12) of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa, as well as the National Health Act's recognition of the right to refuse medical treatment.
  • This directive is intended as a clear expression of my wishes and should be respected by medical practitioners in accordance with established medical ethics and the guidelines of the Health Professions Council of South Africa (HPCSA).
