This page is for information only and does not form part of the Will.

{# --- Who can be a witness ------------------------------------------------ #}

Who Can Be a Witness

  • Any person aged 14 years or older who is competent to give evidence in a court of law.
  • A witness must not be a beneficiary named in this Will, nor the spouse of a beneficiary.
  • A witness must not be the person appointed as executor in this Will, unless they are also a beneficiary (which would disqualify them under the rule above).
  • Both witnesses must be present at the same time when the testator signs.
{# --- How to sign --------------------------------------------------------- #}

How to Sign This Will

  • The testator must sign the Will on the signature page in the presence of both witnesses simultaneously.
  • Both witnesses must then sign the signature page in the presence of the testator and each other.
  • The testator and both witnesses must initial every page of the Will (in the spaces provided at the bottom of each page).
  • All signatures and initials must be in wet ink (blue or black ballpoint pen recommended).
{# --- Important requirements ---------------------------------------------- #}

Important Requirements

  • Wet ink only: This Will must be signed with a physical pen. Electronic or digital signatures are not valid under the Wills Act 7 of 1953.
  • Original document: Only the signed original is legally valid. Photocopies, scans, or digital copies cannot be used for probate.
  • Safe storage: Store the signed original in a fireproof safe, with your attorney, or at the Master of the High Court. Inform your executor of the storage location.
  • Previous wills: If you have an existing will, this new will revokes it upon valid execution. Destroy all copies of the previous will to avoid confusion.
{# --- Do NOT list --------------------------------------------------------- #}

Do NOT

  • Do NOT use correction fluid (Tipp-Ex) on any page. If a correction is needed, draw a single line through the error, write the correction, and have all parties initial the change.
  • Do NOT staple, pin, or attach anything to the Will after signing.
  • Do NOT remove or rearrange pages after signing.
  • Do NOT sign the Will unless both witnesses are physically present.
  • Do NOT use this Will if any pages are missing or damaged.
